Retirement Planning Guides

Contributions to 401(k)s and traditional IRAs are tax-deferred, meaning you don't pay taxes on the money until you withdraw it in retirement. Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals under certain conditions.

By age 30, aim to have one year’s salary saved. By 40, aim for three times your salary. Adjust your strategy based on retirement goals, lifestyle, and income growth.

Many employers offer matching contributions to your 401(k). Contribute enough to get the full match, as it's essentially free money for your retirement.

Explore options like HSAs, SEP IRAs, and solo 401(k)s if you're self-employed or want additional ways to save for retirement.
